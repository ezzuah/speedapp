REFLECTION

One of these three phenomena is likely to happen when light rays hit a surface. Either the rays are absorbed, transmitted or reflected. Reflection is the turning back of light rays from the boundary of a medium. Reflection is in two forms depending on the surface reflecting the rays.


  1. REGULAR REFLECTION: this is the reflection of a parallel beam of light in the same direction by a smooth surface.

  2. IRREGULAR REFLECTION: this is the reflection of a parallel beam of light in different direction by a rough surface.

LAWS OF REFLECTION

There are two laws governing reflection of light.


  1. The incident ray, reflected ray and the normal to the reflecting surface lie in the same plane. The normal as shown in the picture below is an imaginary line drawn perpendicularly to the reflecting surface.

  2. The angle of incidence (i) is equal to the angle of reflection (r).


Reflection in Mirrors

There are three types of mirror. They are:

  1. Plane mirro

  2. Converging mirror

  3. Diverging mirror


Plane mirror

The figure below shows how images are formed in a plane mirror. The image formed by a plane mirror has the following characteristics.


  1. Virtual (that is; not real)

  2. Upright or erect

  3. The same size as image

  4. The same distance from mirror just like the object

  5. Laterally inverted (left part becomes right and right becomes left).

Plane mirrors are usually used as dressing mirrors. They are also used in periscopes.


Curved Mirrors

These are mirrors that are taken from a hollow spherical object (not a circle). The two types of curved mirrors are concave or converging mirror and convex or diverging mirror. If the inner surface of the sphere is the reflecting surface then the mirror is known as the converging mirror and if the outer surface is the reflecting surface then it is known as diverging mirror.


Terminologies Associated with Curved Mirrors

Center of Curvature; this is the center of the sphere of which the mirror forms a part. The figure on the right shows the centre of curvature c.

Radius of Curvature; this is the distance from the center of the mirror to the center of curvature.

Principal Axis; this is an imaginary line that runs through the centre of curvature and the centre of the mirror (point v).

Principal Focus; this is the point on the principal axis where rays parallel to the principal axis converge (or tend to diverge). The principal focuses (f) of the converging and diverging mirrors are shown under the pictures on curved mirrors.

Focal Length; this is the distance from the centre (point v) of the reflecting surface to the principal focus. For curved mirrors with small angular surface, the focal length is one–half the radius of curvature

2f = c or f = c/2.


Property of image formed in a diverging mirror

Images formed by a convex mirror have the following properties.

  1. The image is virtual

  2. It is formed behind the mirror

  3. Erect or upright

  4. Reduced or diminished

Since they give a wider field of view and their image is always uptight, they are used as driving mirrors. It is also used in supermarkets to check shoplifting.


Property of image formed a converging mirror

The properties of the image formed by a concave mirror depend on the position of the image in front of the mirror. In the lens diagrams that follow, o, represents the object and, i, represents the image.

When object is placed between the principal focus and the mirror:


  1. It is virtual

  2. Erect

  3. Magnified (enlarged)

  4. Located behind the mirror


When object is placed at the principal focus


  1. image is formed at infinity


When object is placed between the centre of curvature and the principal focus


  1. the image is real

  2. formed beyond the centre of curvature

  3. it is inverted

  4. enlarged


When object is placed at the centre of curvature


  1. image is real

  2. same size as object

  3. located at the centre of curvature

  4. inverted


When object is placed beyond the centre of curvature


  1. image is real

  2. it is inverted

  3. it is reduced

  4. located between the centre of curvature and the principal focus


When object is placed at infinity


  1. image is real

  2. inverted

  3. image is diminished

  4. located at the principal focus


Converging mirrors are used as shaving mirrors.
