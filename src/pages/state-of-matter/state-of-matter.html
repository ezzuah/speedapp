STATES OF MATTER



Physical and Chemical Changes

Change is any difference in either the physical or chemical property of a substance. The physical properties of a substance include, color, density, solubility, melting point, boiling point, hardness, liquid, solid or gas, taste, etc. and the chemical properties are, acidity, oxidizing or reducing agents, metals or non metals, etc.


Physical Change: it is any change in an original substance that does not lead to the production of new substances. However, there could be a temporary change in the physical properties of the substance. For example, when candle wax is heated, it melts (solid to liquid, and the density will decrease). On cooling, the original candle wax is obtained again. We notice that, there have been a change, but the change did not result in the production of a new substance.


Chemical Change: this is any change in an original substance which leads to the production of new substance(s). For example, when nail is allowed to rust completely, the process can not be reversed to obtain the original nail again.
