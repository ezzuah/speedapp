WORK, ENERGY AND POWER

In everyday language we use the word work to describe any muscular or mental effort we expend in any activity. In physics, the word work has a different meaning. If a force acts on a body and moves through a distance, then we say work has been done in the scientific sense.


It follows that, if you carry a 100kg load on your shoulder without moving, then no work has been done. You only exerted an upward force to counteract the downward force the weight of the body exerts.


Work is defined as the product of force and distance and the SI unit is the Joule (J). Work is measured by two factors:

  1. The magnitude of the applied force and

  2. The distance the load is moved through by the applied force.


Mathematically, Work is defined as:

W = Fs;


where F = applied force and s = distance moved through by body.

Solution

Energy

This is defined as the ability to do work. Energy has units just as work which is the Joule.

  1. Kinetic energy; this is the energy possessed by a moving body.

  2. Potential energy; this is the energy possessed by a body by virtue of its height.

  3. Thermal energy; energy associated with heat generation or transfer.

  4. Sound energy; this is in the form of vibrations from a sound source example loud speaker.

  5. Nuclear energy; the energy associated with the fusion (uniting) or fission (splitting) of atomic nucleus.

  6. Electrical energy; this is the energy generated from electrical sources.

  7. Light energy; energy given off by a luminous body.


Transformation Of Energy

The total energy of the earth is constant. This means that energy can not be created or destroyed but it is transformed into other forms. For example, electrical energy that comes from Akosombo into our PC’s is converted into sound, heat and light energies. The principle of conservation of energy states that the total energy of a system remains constant.

POWER

This is defined as the rate at which work is done. The SI unit is Watt (J/s).


Mathematically, it is given as


P = Work Done
time

Let us analyse this practical scenario. Two 15N weight blocks can be carried to a distance of 4m in these two ways.

  1. A force of 30N will be applied to carry both blocks at the same time to the 4m destination. In which case the work done W = 30N x 4m = 120J

  2. A force of 15N will be applied to carry a single block at a time. In which case the distance to be covered by the blocks will be 4m for each block and it will amount to 8m for both. Therefore the work done in this case W = 15N x 8m = 120J.


It is obvious that the time that will be used in case 1 will be less than that used in case 2. Therefore, the power or rate of doing the work in case 1 will be higher than case 2.
