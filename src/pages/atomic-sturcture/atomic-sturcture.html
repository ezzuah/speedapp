ATOMIC STRUCTURE

There are many materials around us. All these materials are termed matter because they have mass and volume. By definition matter is any substance that has mass and occupies space. Examples are water, air, stone, wood etc.


All forms of matter are made up of tiny particles called atom. The atom is the smallest divisible unit of an element that can take part in a chemical reaction. The atom is said to be divisible in the sense that it can be broken down into its primary components which are the electrons, protons and neutrons.


The atom has a central nucleus which contains the protons and neutrons. The electrons reside in orbits known as energy shells found around the nucleus. The proton has a charge of +1, neutron has no charge and electron has a charge of -1. The number of protons and electrons in an atom are equal hence, the atom as a whole is neutral.


However, the presence of the positively charged protons in the nucleus makes the nucleus (of the atom) positively charged. The mass of a proton and neutron is 1u, whereas that of an electron is 1/1840u which is negligible. It follows that the nucleus bears almost the total mass of an atom. Elements and Compounds

Scientists have discovered over hundred different types of atoms and these atoms make up the things we see around us. But the atoms making up a substance may be the same or different. If atoms making up a substance are the same, then the substance is an element. Elements are fundamental substances made up of only one kind of atoms and hence, they can not be broken down into other substances by any chemical means.


Since there are over hundred different types of atoms, it follows that there over hundred different elements. Some of the elements discovered are Iron, lead, oxygen, nitrogen, etc. Scientists use a short hand known as chemical symbol to represent each element. For instance, oxygen is represented by O, Nitrogen by N chlorine by Cl, and many more.


When different elements combine chemically they form compounds. A compound is a substance formed from the chemical combination of two or more elements. Compounds have different properties (behaviour) from their constituents. For example, Chlorine as an element is a poisonous gas, but it is a component in table salt which is not poisonous. This confirms the fact that compounds have different properties from their constituents.


Atomic Number, Mass Number and Isotope

Atoms of the same element have the same number of protons and atoms of different elements have different numbers of protons. The number of protons in an atom is its atomic number and it is denoted by the letter Z. The sum of the number of protons and neutrons in an atom is the mass number of that atom (or element) and it is denoted by A.


The mass number of atoms of a particular element may vary because the neutron numbers in atoms of a particular element may not be the same. Atoms that have the same atomic number(number of protons) but different mass numbers are called isotopes.


The picture above shows the three isotopes of hydrogen element. It is obvious that isotopy is as a result of difference in neutron numbers. In a nut shell, the number of protons determines the identity of an element and the mass number determines the particular isotope of the element, if it has more than one.


Scientists perform calculations which may involve the atomic mass of an atom. However, it will be appropriate to represent an element with the average mass of its various isotopes in calculations than to use the mass of just one of its isotopes. The average mass of all the masses of the various isotopes of an element is the relative atomic mass of the element. Relative atomic mass is denoted by Ar.

Atomic Mass Unit (amu)

It is appropriate to determine the masses of most substances such as cup, book, bottle, etc in grams or kilograms. However, the gram and the kilogram scale are very inappropriate for the atomic particle. For example, the mass of 1 hydrogen atom is 1.67 x 10-24 grams. This is a small number and that a new mass unit is required on the atomic level.


Scientists have therefore defined a mass unit for the atomic particles and this is the atomic mass unit. The atomic mass unit is defined as 1/12th the mass of carbon-12 atom. The carbon-12 atom has 6 protons and six neutrons therefore its mass on the amu scale is 12u. The atomic mass unit is denoted by the letter u.


Electron Shell

The path along which the electron moves is known as electron shell or orbit. Each orbit can accommodate a certain maximum number of electrons. The maximum number of electrons an orbit (shell) can accommodate is given by the formula 2n2. Where n is an integer and denotes the shell number.


The shells are assigned numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and named K, L, M, N, O, respectively, as we move away from the nucleus. The outermost shell in an atom is called the valence shell and the electrons that occupy that shell are the valence electrons . The inner shell(s) are called core shell(s) and the electrons that occupy them are the core electrons.

Electron Configuration (Arrangements)

Electron Configuration refers to the arrangements of electrons in an atom. Shells that are closer to the nucleus are lower in energy and electrons will occupy them before any other. For example, experiments have shown that hydrogen has one electron and this electron occupies only the K shell. The other shells like L, M, N and O are present in the hydrogen atom but they are empty.


That is, K shell is of a lower energy than the L shell, and the L shell is of a lower energy than the M shell and so on. In short, the K shell must be fully filled before the L, before the M, the N and so on.

The electronic configurations for helium and neon are referred to as the duplet and octet structures respectively. These two configurations are the most stable electronic arrangements because the outer shells are fully filled.
