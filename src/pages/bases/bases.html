BASES


The three theories that define an acid (as stated in the previous chapter) also apply to bases.


  1. Arrhenius theory: A base is any substance that dissolves in water to produce OH- ions.


  2. Bronsted-Lowry theory: A base is a proton acceptor


  3. Lewis theory: A base is an electron pair donor.


Bases can be thought of as chemically opposite to acids, because as acids are donating more protons to make a solution acidic, bases will be accepting these protons, reducing the acidity of the solution. The reaction between an acid and a base is called neutralization reaction.


Strong and weak bases

A base that ionizes completely in water when it is dissolved is known as strong base, example, NaOH, Ca(OH)2, KOH, etc; whereas a weak base is a base that ionizes partially when dissolved in water. Examples are Ammonium hydroxide, Urea, etc.


Properties of bases

They;

  1. Have bitter taste.

  2. Have a ph greater than 7.

  3. Are very caustic if concentrated.

  4. Turns red litmus paper blue.

  5. Has a soapy feel.

  6. Reacts with acids to form salt and water.

HNO3 + NaOH NaNO3 + H2O

  1. Reacts with ammonium salt to form ammonia


NH4Cl + NaOH NaCl + NH3 + H2O

NaOH Manufacturing

Diaphragm cell process – is an electrolytic (electrolysis) process which uses a steel cathode, titanium anode and concentrated sodium chloride solution (brine). The products of the electrolysis are NaOH, Cl2, O2 and H2. NaOH reacts readily with Cl2. This reaction is not needed; hence it is prevented by separating the anode area from the cathode area by a porous diaphragm or membrane often made of asbestos fiber and polymers. The brine is introduced into the anode compartment and flows through the diaphragm into the cathode compartment.



The solution level in the anode compartment must always be higher than that of the cathode compartment to ensure that the electrolyte (brine) flows from the anode compartment to the cathode compartment. The NaOH is usually concentrated to 50% and the salt removed. This is done using an evaporative process. The salt separated from the caustic brine can be used to saturate diluted brine. The chlorine contains oxygen and is purified by liquefaction and evaporation.


Uses of some bases

NaOH: Sodium hydroxide is used in the refining of alumina containing ore (bauxite), soap production, paper making, production of biodiesel, manufacture of synthetic fabrics, etc.


KOH: is use for the manufacturing of soap, removal of grease and oils.

Ammonia: it is used for the preparation of fertilizers.

Calcium hydroxide; Ca(OH)2: it is used as an additive in the production of cement. Calcium hydroxide can be used to raise the pH of acidic soils.

Calcium oxide (CaO) is a key ingredient for the process of making cement.

Safety

Solid bases or solutions containing high concentrations of strong bases may cause chemical burns, permanent injury or scarring, and blindness.
