ALKANOIC ACID (CARBOXYLIC ACIDS)

Although there are other types of organic acids, the most common organic acids belong to a group called the carboxylic acids. Many fruits and vegetables contain carboxylic acids. One such acid is the familiar citric acid found in lemons, oranges and other citrus fruits. Tartaric acid is found in grapes. The functional group of carboxylic acid is the carboxyl group and is represented as.



Carboxylic acids have the general formula CnH(2n+1)COOH. Carboxylic acids react with alcohols to produce esters (alkylalkanoate). It also reacts with sodium hydrogen trioxocarbonate (IV) NaHCO3 to liberate carbon dioxide (CO2).

Uses of Methanoic Acid

It is used as a drying agent when making textiles.

It is used to remove calcium trioxocarbonate (IV), chalk, deposits in kettle.

It is also used as a starting chemical to produce other chemicals.


Uses of Ethanoic Acis

It is used in laundries for brightening colours.

Used as a solvent to dissolve other carbon compounds.

It is used as vinegar for food flavouring.

Used for cleaning dirty metal surfaces in homes.

It is also used for the production of drugs and other chemicals.

Cellulose ethanoate which is used for the manufacturing of liquors, varnishes, cinema films and rayon are made from ethanoic acid.
