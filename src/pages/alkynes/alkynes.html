ALKYNES (CnH2n–2)

Alkynes are unsaturated hydrocarbons that contain at least a single carbon– carbon triple bond. They have the functional group and do not exhibit geometric isomerism due to their linear structure. Alkyne decolourise bromine water and they have the general formula CnH2n–2 where n=2, 3, 4… Below is the structural formula of the first three members of the group.











Nomenclature

In naming alkynes, the usual IUPAC rules for hydrocarbons are followed. The suffix ‘ane’ in alkanes is replaced by ‘yne’. The position of the triple bond along the chain is indicated by a number as in the alkene nomenclature.











Reactions in Alkynes

Hydrogenation

H—C≡ C—H + 2H2 CH3—CH3


Bromination

H—C≡ C—H + 2Br2 CHBr2—CHBr2


Combustion

This is the process of burning a substance in the presence of oxygen. All hydrocarbons yield carbon dioxide and water when they undergo combustion.


Example
