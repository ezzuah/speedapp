MOTION

If you leave your house or dormitory to the school’s computer lab, you have covered some distance. You may also leave the lab for a different class. In each case, you are changing your location. During the time that you are making these changes your body is in motion.


Motion is the change in position of a body with respect to time. This branch of physics is called kinematics in that; problems pertaining to motions are solved without considering the forces that are causing the motion. The other branch that considers the forces causing the motion is called dynamics.

Types of Motion

  1. Linear Motion: this is a motion in which the body moves in a straight line.


  2. Oscillatory or Simple Harmonic motion: the body moves to and fro, like the swinging pendulum.


  3. Rotational motion: this is a motion in which the body spins on an axis. Example, rotation of a fan or the spinning wheels of a vehicle.


  4. Circular motion: this is a kind of motion in which the body moves in a circular path.


  5. Translational Motion: this is a form of motion that does not involve any form of rotation.


  6. Random Motion: this is a kind of motion in which the body moves haphazardly. Like the motion of gas particles.


Below are some quantities that you should know and understand.


Displacement/Distance: displacement is the distance measured in a specified direction. Note that distance has no direction. For instance, Kofi walked 20m today. Kofi can cover 20m distance but he may not change his position.


Reason: He can walk 10m away from his position and 10m back to his original position. He has made 20m but he did not change his position. Therefore displacement and distance have two different meanings in physics.


Velocity/Speed: this is the distance covered by a body in a unit time in a specified direction. Note that the difference between velocity and speed is that velocity has direction whilst speed has not. Just like the displacement–distance illustration above. The SI unit of velocity is meter per second (m/s).

Where v = velocity, s = distance travelled and t = time taken to travel the distance s.


Acceleration: Only under very unusual circumstances can a body travel at constant speed. If we drive through town, we continually stop for red traffic lights. When they change to green we start on our way again.


Through this stopping and starting, we change our velocity at different rates. In physics, the rate at which a body changes its velocity is known as acceleration. If a body is reducing its velocity we say it is decelerating or retarding and if it is increasing it, it is accelerating. The SI unit of acceleration is meter per second squared (m/s2).


Mathematically, acceleration,

Acceleration, a = v - u

Where v = final velocity, u = initial velocity and t = time.

Velocity time graph

If a body is travelling with varying acceleration, the equations of motion become a bit confusing and cumbersome. In such cases, the velocity–time graph may proof very useful. This is a graph with velocity plotted on the vertical and time on the horizontal coordinates.
