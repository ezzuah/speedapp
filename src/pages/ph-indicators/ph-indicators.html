pH AND INDICATORS

pH is a measure of the acidity or alkalinity (Basicity) of a solution. Note that, acidity of a solution depends on the concentration of H+ ions in the solution. Therefore pH can also be defined with respect to the concentration of this ion as the measure of H3O+ ion concentration in a solution. Scientist have defined a scale on which pH is measured. It ranges from 0 to 14. On the pH scale, a pH value less than 7 means acidity, 7 is neutral and above 7 is alkalinity. The lower the pH value, the more acidic a solution is and the greater the pH value the more basic a solution is. For example a solution with a pH of 3 is more acidic than a solution with a pH of 4.2.



The pH scale


To determine the pH of a solution using

  1. Universal indicator; put about 5cm3 of the solution whose pH is to be determined in a beaker. Add two to three drops of the indicator solution to the solution in the beaker and shake well for a colour change to appear. Compare the colour that appears with a standard pH colour chart to find a good match. The pH range of that colour is the pH of the solution.


  2. The pH meter; Dip the electrode of the meter in the solution and the pH is displayed on the scale as shown on the right.



Indicators

These are substances that give a particular colour depending on the pH of the solution in which they are put.


Colours of Indicators in Acid – Base medium

Indicator Colour in acidic medium Colour in basic medium
Phenolphthalein Colourless Red
Methyl orange Red Yellow
Litmus Red Blue


Note that acidic solution turns blue litmus red but it has no effect on red litmus. Similarly, basic solutions turn red litmus blue, but they have no effect on blue litmus.


Buffer Solution

This is a solution whose pH does not change appreciably when small amount of acid or base is added to it. A buffer can be made from a weak acid and its salt. Example is a solution containing acetic acid and sodium acetate. Blood is also known to be buffer a solution.
