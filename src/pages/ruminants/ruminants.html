RUMINANTS

The goat, sheep and cattle are another group of farm animals known as ruminants. Ruminantsare animals with four–chamber stomach.


Types of Cattle

Cattle are grouped based on the services they provide to man.

  1. Beef Cattle; these are cattle reared purposely for their meat. They are fast growing and puts on a lot of weight.

  2. Diary Cattle; these are reared purposely for milk production.

  3. Dual purpose; these breeds are good producers of both meat and milk.

  4. Work Type; these are bulls and bullocks used on farm land to plough and cart farm goods.

Systems of Management in Livestock

Like poultry, ruminants have three management systems, they are


  1. Extensive System; this is the system of management where the animals are sometimes provided with a house. They are left to graze over a wide area under the supervision of herdsmen.

  2. Semi–intensive; under this system, the animals are provided with permanent houses. The farmer provides part of their food and they top it up with grazing.

  3. Intensive; under this system, the animals are kept in a permanent house 24–7 and the farmer provides their entire food requirement.
